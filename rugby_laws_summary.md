# Summary of the Laws of Rugby Union

The Laws of Rugby Union are governed globally by **World Rugby**. The laws are designed to ensure a fair, continuous, and safe contest for possession while maintaining the sport's core character.

## 1. Objective and Core Principles
The fundamental objective of the game is for two teams of 15 players to score as many points as possible by carrying, passing, kicking, and grounding the ball. 
- **Continuity:** Play should be as continuous as possible.
- **The Contest:** The defining feature of rugby is the physical contest for possession, which occurs at tackles, rucks, mauls, scrums, and lineouts.
- **Passing Backwards:** The ball may only be passed or knocked backwards or laterally. Throwing or dropping the ball forward (towards the opponent's dead-ball line) is illegal.

## 2. Match Duration and Teams
- **Time:** A standard match consists of two halves of 40 minutes each, separated by a half-time break of up to 15 minutes.
- **Players:** Each team fields 15 players on the pitch, typically split into 8 "forwards" (who specialize in set-pieces and physical contests) and 7 "backs" (who specialize in open play, pace, and kicking).

## 3. Scoring System
There are four ways to score points in rugby union:
- **Try (5 points):** Awarded when an attacking player applies downward pressure on the ball within the opponent's in-goal area (try zone).
- **Conversion (2 points):** After a try is scored, the scoring team attempts to kick the ball over the crossbar and between the uprights from a line parallel to where the try was scored.
- **Penalty Goal (3 points):** When a team is awarded a penalty for a major infringement, they may opt to kick for goal.
- **Drop Goal (3 points):** Any player may attempt to score during open play by dropping the ball onto the ground and kicking it through the posts on the half-volley.

## 4. Key Gameplay Phases

### The Tackle
A tackle occurs when the ball carrier is held by one or more opponents and brought to the ground. 
- The tackler must immediately release the tackled player and roll away.
- The tackled player must immediately pass or release the ball.
- Arriving players must enter the tackle area from their own side (through the "gate") and stay on their feet.

### Rucks and Mauls
When a tackle is made and players from both teams arrive and bind over the ball on the ground, a **ruck** is formed. Players cannot use their hands in a ruck; they must use their feet to hook the ball back.
A **maul** occurs when the ball carrier is held by one or more opponents, and at least one teammate binds onto the ball carrier. The ball is off the ground, and teams attempt to physically drive each other backward.

## 5. Set Pieces (Restarts)

### The Scrum
A scrum is a physical contest used to restart play after a minor infringement, such as a "knock-on" (dropping the ball forward) or a forward pass.
- Both packs of eight forwards bind together and push against each other.
- The non-offending team's scrum-half rolls the ball into the "tunnel" between the two front rows, and the hookers attempt to sweep it backward with their feet.

### The Lineout
When the ball goes out of bounds over the touchline, play is restarted with a lineout.
- Forwards from both teams line up parallel to each other.
- The team that did not kick or carry the ball out throws it straight down the middle of the two lines.
- Players are lifted by their teammates to catch the ball in the air.

## 6. Foul Play and Sanctions
World Rugby strictly penalizes actions that violate safety or fair play.
- **Free Kicks & Scrums:** Awarded for minor technical offenses (e.g., forward pass).
- **Penalties:** Awarded for major infractions like offside, high tackles, dangerous play, or intentionally killing the ball at the breakdown. The offended team can kick for goal, kick to touch (and retain the resulting lineout), or tap and run.
- **Cards:** Serious foul play or repeated infringements result in a Yellow Card (10 minutes in the "sin bin") or a Red Card (permanent expulsion from the match).
