**Basic 1-3-3-1 attacking shape**


The JSON below gives an example of a basic 1-3-3-1 attacking shape. 

The home team are attacking towards the top of the pitch.

This is at a ruck on the left hand side of the pitch. The first forward 3-pod (8,1,2) have taken the ball up into the ruck. The next forward 3-pod (4,5,3) are standing ready to carry. The 7 and 6 are the edge forwards (the ones in the 1-3-3-1) and are holding width. The backs are lined up in an attacking line 10-12-13-14, with the 11 and 15 offering more depth.

The ball is passed from the 9 to the 5, who takes the ball at pace. The rest of the attackers move up in support. The 9 runs a slight cheat line, ahead of the ball, trying to anticipate where the ruck will form. The first pod take a few steps back so they are offering a carry option on the blind side.

The ball moves back out the ruck quickly to the 9, who passed flat to the 10 moving at pace. The ball is then moved along the line 12, 13 and 14 carries into contact. Number 7 and 13 go into the ruck. The players then start to form a 1-3-3-1 shape the other way, ready to move back.

This is just to give an idea of how rugby flows in 1-3-3-1 shapes. It is not a prescriptive move. It probably moves a bit too quickly, so we should extend it over a longer number of frames.
